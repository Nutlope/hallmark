# Flarepin

## Register

brand

## Product Purpose

Error-tracking SaaS for backend developers. Captures uncaught exceptions, slow queries, and async-stack issues in Node, Python, and Go. Self-hosted or managed.

## Users

On-call backend engineers and small SRE teams. They live in terminals and IDEs, not in dashboards. They have used Sentry, Datadog, or Rollbar and either churned (too noisy, too marketing-heavy) or never adopted (too expensive at small scale).

Frequency: paged at 2am, several times a month. Daily passive scanning during work hours.

Context: 27-inch monitor at 11pm, dim room, headphones on, half a stack trace in their head already.

## Voice

Technical, sharp, no marketing fluff. Reads like a `man` page that has been rewritten by someone who has feelings about it.

Voice words: exact, terse, on-call.

Forbidden phrases: "empower", "unlock", "delight", "seamless", "next-generation", "AI-powered", "trusted by", "world-class".

## Strategic Principles

- Show the product, not promises. Stack traces, latency numbers, query plans go on the page.
- Honesty over inflation. We have 120 beta teams; we say 120, not "thousands of teams".
- Self-hosted is a real option, not a footnote. Backend developers know what `docker compose up` is.
- Pricing on the page. Always.

## Anti-References

- Sentry post-2022 (purple-gradient marketing dilution).
- Datadog (enterprise-sales decks dressed as a landing page).
- "Trusted by Acme, Globex, Initech" logo walls.
- Hero metric template: one giant gradient number above a fake testimonial.
- AI-cream / pastel-violet observability surfaces.
